# Design Brief — Lupine Field Coffee

## Brand Overview
Lupine Field Coffee is a single-origin specialty roaster in Missoula, MT. They source
directly from farms in Ethiopia, Colombia, and Guatemala and publish full pricing
transparency on every bag. Founded by a former NPR journalist who wanted coffee that
was both excellent and honest about its supply chain.

## Visual Identity

**Primary colors:**
- Deep forest green `#1F3A2E`
- Warm cream `#F4EFE6`
- Charcoal `#1A1A1A` (text, never pure black)

**Secondary/accent colors:**
- Burnt orange `#C25A28` (CTAs, links, harvest tags)
- Lupine purple `#5B4B8A` (used sparingly — limited release callouts only)

**Typography preferences:**
- Headlines: a serif with personality (think GT Sectra, Tiempos, or similar). Editorial, not luxury.
- Body: a clean sans (Inter or similar). Legible at 16px.
- No script fonts. No display fonts in body copy.

**Logo/mark notes:** Wordmark "LUPINE FIELD" in the serif, all caps, with a small lupine flower silhouette as the icon mark. Mark works as favicon and avatar; wordmark for header. Available in green and cream — never on the orange.

## Aesthetic Direction

Editorial. Calm. Documentary photography. The coffee bag is the hero, but always
shot in context — on a rough wooden table, against a kitchen scene, in someone's
hands. Never floating product shots on white.

Think: a thoughtful magazine spread, not an ecommerce store. Generous whitespace.
Long-form blocks of text are okay if the writing is good (and it will be).

**Mood keywords:** editorial, grounded, transparent, deliberate, Mountain West (without the kitsch)

**Reference stores (URLs):**
- driftaway.coffee (transparency framing — but ours should feel less corporate)
- yallah-coffee.co.uk (typography and editorial restraint)
- onyxcoffeelab.com (product page depth — but our voice is quieter)

**What to avoid:**
- Coffee-bro tropes: chalkboard fonts, "FRESH ROASTED!" badges, coffee bean borders
- Audiophile-style cupping notes that sound like wine reviews ("notes of jasmine and stone fruit with a buttery finish")
- Sale mechanics, urgency banners, "ONLY 3 LEFT" messaging
- Stock photography of any kind

## Photography & Media Style
All product photography is documentary — the bag in use, in someone's kitchen, on
the roastery floor, on the farm. Natural light, slight grain. The merchant has a
strong photo library already; we're not commissioning new shoots.

Lifestyle imagery includes the farms (we have rights to farm photos from origin trips).
Use these on origin-specific product pages and the About page.

Video: short loops only, no narrated brand films. A 6-second loop of beans falling
from a roaster, used on the homepage hero, would be ideal.

## Homepage Vision
The homepage should feel like opening a thoughtful magazine. Hero is a single
photograph + one declarative line — no carousel, no overlay text. Below that, a
short editorial intro to the brand (3 sentences max), then the current featured
single-origin with its actual purchase price callout, then the subscription pitch,
then social proof, then email signup.

The hero message of the moment is: **"Coffee with a paper trail."**

## Key Pages Needed

- [x] Collection pages (Whole Bean, Brew Gear, Subscriptions)
- [x] Product pages (single template, but origin-specific PDP needs map + farm bio)
- [x] About (founder story + transparency methodology)
- [x] Custom: "The Ledger" — public price-per-pound history page
- [x] Custom: "Field Notes" — quarterly subscription landing page

## Mobile Priorities
- The product page on mobile is where 70% of conversions happen — prioritize it
- Hero image must crop intelligently to mobile portrait (no critical content at edges)
- Add to cart should be sticky on mobile PDP

## Accessibility Notes
- All text on green must be cream — never orange on green (contrast fail)
- Body text minimum 16px on mobile
- All imagery needs descriptive alt text — this is a transparency brand, accessibility is a values match
